I am a PhD student in the [biostatistics department](https://publichealth.berkeley.edu/academics/biostatistics/) at UC Berkeley working with [Mark van der Laan](https://vanderlaan-lab.org/). My research interests revolve around personalized medicine, causal inference and machine learning. Most of my current work involves causal inference with times-series, optimal individualized treatment, online learning, reinforcement learning, and adaptive sequential designs. 

I am a founding core developer of the [tlverse](https://tlverse.org/) project, the software ecosystem for targeted learning. During my graduate studies, I enjoyed numerious collaborations with [Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation](https://www.gatesfoundation.org/), [Gilead Sciences](https://www.gilead.com/), [Kaiser Permanente](https://healthy.kaiserpermanente.org/northern-california/front-door), and [TGen](https://www.tgen.org/).
